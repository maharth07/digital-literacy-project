# Social Media Checklist for College Students

Look, college is already stressful enough without having a poorly timed tweet come back to haunt you. Here's a no-nonsense checklist to help you stay smart online without overthinking every single post.

---

## Things You Should Definitely Be Doing ✅

| # | The Do |
|---|--------|
| 1 | **Pause before you post.** Seriously, just a two-second gut check — "would I cringe if my professor or a future boss saw this?" If the answer is even a maybe, sit on it. |
| 2 | **Clean up your profile every few months.** That bio you wrote freshman year? Probably time for an update. A decent photo and a line about what you're studying goes a long way. |
| 3 | **Be a decent human in the comments.** Disagreeing with someone is totally fine — just do it like a person who has to show their face in public someday. |
| 4 | **Actually check your privacy settings.** Not once when you made the account — regularly. Platforms love quietly resetting these after updates. |
| 5 | **Credit people for their work.** Found a quote, a photo, or an idea you want to share? Tag the creator or mention the source. It takes five seconds and it's just the right thing to do. |
| 6 | **Use these platforms to actually grow.** Follow people in your field, join groups related to your major, shoot your shot on LinkedIn. Social media can be genuinely useful if you treat it that way. |
| 7 | **Verify stuff before you share it.** If a headline sounds wild, it probably is. Check it against a real news source before you repost it to your 600 followers. |

---

## Things That Will Come Back to Bite You ❌

| # | The Don't |
|---|-----------|
| 1 | **Don't put your personal details out there for anyone to see.** Your address, phone number, daily schedule — none of that needs to be public. You genuinely never know who's watching. |
| 2 | **Don't post yourself doing something you could get in serious trouble for.** Whether it's academic or legal trouble, that content lives forever even after you delete it. |
| 3 | **Don't be that person who subtweets, mocks, or piles on others online.** It looks bad, it feels bad later, and campuses are smaller than you think. |
| 4 | **Don't pass off someone else's stuff as yours.** Screenshots, articles, photos — always give credit. Getting called out for this publicly is genuinely mortifying. |
| 5 | **Don't announce to the internet when you're home alone or traveling.** "Finally leaving for spring break!!" with your location tagged is not the move. |
| 6 | **Don't assume old posts are buried and forgotten.** They're not. People screenshot things. Do a quick scroll through your own history once in a while and clean house. |
| 7 | **Don't let it eat your time without you noticing.** One minute you're checking notifications, next minute it's 2am and you have an 8am class. Set some limits — your GPA will thank you. |

---

*Your digital presence follows you around a lot longer than you'd expect. Treat it like it matters — because it actually does.*
