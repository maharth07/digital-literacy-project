# Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist
### A Cybersecurity Guide for College Students in India

---

## General Online Safety

- [ ] **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account (email, social media, college portal).
      Use a passphrase like `BlueSky@Rain2024` instead of simple words. Never reuse
      the same password across multiple platforms.

- [ ] **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — especially
      your Google account, email, and banking apps. This adds a second layer of
      verification even if your password is stolen.

- [ ] **Never click on suspicious links** received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email — even if
      they appear to come from a known contact. Always verify by calling the person
      directly before clicking.

- [ ] **Keep your devices and apps updated.** Software updates often contain critical
      security patches. Enable automatic updates on your phone and laptop.

- [ ] **Avoid using public Wi-Fi** (college canteen, railway station, malls) for banking,
      email logins, or any sensitive activity. If you must use public Wi-Fi, use a VPN.

- [ ] **Log out of shared computers** completely after use in labs, libraries, or cyber
      cafés. Clear the browser history and saved passwords before leaving.

---

## Financial / UPI Safety *(Mandatory Tips)*

- [ ] **Understand the difference between "Send" and "Receive" on UPI apps.**
      You NEVER need to enter your UPI PIN to *receive* money. If someone tells you
      to enter your PIN to accept a payment — it is a scam. Hang up immediately.

- [ ] **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV** with anyone — including people who
      claim to be bank employees, customer care representatives, or "Google Pay support."
      No legitimate bank or app will ever ask for these details.

- [ ] **Verify UPI payment requests before approving.** When you receive a payment
      request on PhonePe, Google Pay, or Paytm, read it carefully — a "request" means
      money will leave your account, not enter it.

- [ ] **Set a daily UPI transaction limit** in your banking app to cap potential losses in
      case of fraud.

---

## Social Media Safety

- [ ] **Think before you post.** Never share your college ID, Aadhaar number, hostel
      room number, daily schedule, or travel plans on public social media profiles.

- [ ] **Set your social media accounts to private** and review your followers/friends list
      regularly. Reject requests from people you do not know in real life.

- [ ] **Do not accept "job offers" or "scholarship opportunities"** received via Instagram
      DMs, WhatsApp forwards, or Telegram groups that ask for an upfront registration
      fee or your bank details.

---

## How to Report Cybercrime

If you or someone you know has been a victim of any online fraud or cybercrime:

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — file a complaint online (available 24x7) |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** — call immediately after a financial fraud; early reporting increases the chance of blocking the transaction |
| **Local Police** | Visit your nearest police station and file an FIR under IT Act 2000 |
| **Bank Helpline** | Call your bank immediately to freeze/block your account if credentials are compromised |

> **Important:** Report financial fraud on Helpline 1930 as soon as possible.
> Quick reporting (within minutes to hours) significantly improves the chances of
> recovering the lost amount before it is withdrawn by the attacker.
